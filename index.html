Living far from your family can be lonely, especially during a pandemic. We wanted to make sure our elders weren't.

COVID-19 has affected multiple factors of our lives. For our elders to have to be seperated from their families can be a real struggle, our members wanted to make sure it wasn't.

Our Process

Step 1:

We'll call the local retirement homes for the number of residents residing. Once we have that number, we'll assess it, and determine the number of volunteers that will be required.

Step 2:

Once we have at least a few volunteers, we'll start writing letters. All of our volunteers are encouraged to write letters as if they were going to their own family. This adds a level of connection and emotion to the letter. Our volunteers are also required to use legible handwriting.

Steo 3:

Delivering letters during a pandemic can be tricky, however, we won't stop at this challenge. Since letters are going to be delivered near the Valentine's Day, cards will be written at least a week before the date. Once this is done. We will send them with a small group. This group will wear their masks, and hand them to a [title] which will then properly disinfect the letters for distribution.

Step 1: Recieve Number of People

We'll call the local retirement homes for the number of residents residing. Once we have that number, we'll assess it, and determine the number of volunteers that will be required.

Step 2: Write Letters

Once we have at least a few volunteers, we'll start writing letters. All of our volunteers are encourgaed to write letters as if they were going to their own family. This adds a level of connection and emotion to the letter and recipient. Our volunteers are also required to use legible handwriting.

Step 3: Deliver

Delivering letters during a pandemic can be tricky. This year, a normal visitation would not be possible, this caused us to have to deliver cards outside of the hpme to a staff member. Cards will be completely finished at least one week before the date of delivery, this will allow the cards to stand by, and kill bacteria that could be on them. Cards will them be stored in a large ZipLoc bag where students will not be able to touch. This bag will then be handed to the staff member at the retirement home.
